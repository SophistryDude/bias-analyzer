## Standing Principles Surfaced During the April 2026 9-Axis Rewrite

The profile rewrite process (Batches 1–4, profile-breakdowns.md and revised_profile_breakdown.md) surfaced several framework-level findings that are load-bearing for MediaSentinel's operation and deserve canonical placement in this integration document.

### The Observer-Effect Principle (Musk case)

**When the observer also controls the measurement apparatus, the measurement cannot be treated as independent.**

Elon Musk owns X. X is the primary site of public discussion about Musk. Any measurement of discourse about Musk on X is downstream of decisions by Musk (algorithm, moderation, feature set, trust-and-safety staffing). This is not a neutral observation environment, and the Logic System's separation-of-observation-from-interpretation principle applies at the measurement-instrument level.

This is not a Musk-specific finding. It is a **structural epistemological configuration** that applies to any subject who controls the platform through which they are discussed. The closest historical parallel is Rupert Murdoch controlling Fox News coverage of News Corp, but Murdoch operated one step removed through editors while Musk operates as the primary content creator on his own platform. The configuration is new in scale and directness, and the Logic System's framework has to accommodate it.

**Practical application in MediaSentinel:**
- Flag any subject whose content is primarily delivered via a platform they own or control.
- For such subjects, discount the independence of platform-level metrics (reach, engagement, moderation-compliance) as measurements of their content.
- For such subjects, treat their own claims *about their platform* as having the lowest possible epistemic independence — they are simultaneously the speaker, the subject, and the measurement instrument.
- The label "framework + interest aligned, causation undetermined" is a standing flag for conflicted-interest subjects: when a subject's stated framework and their business/personal interests generate the same outputs, we cannot determine which is driving the position, and the claim should be labeled with that uncertainty rather than attributed to either.

**Category-error detection on the same speaker over time is the core operation of the epistemological classifier.**

This is literally what MediaSentinel's epistemological classifier and claim-tracking system are built to do at scale across thousands of subjects and millions of claims. **The Daily Show was, in effect, a manual coherence-score calculator aired as comedy for 16 years.**

This is the foundational pre-existing case study for the MediaSentinel project. When evaluating whether MediaSentinel is doing something novel, the answer is: it is doing what Stewart did manually, at industrial scale, with every politician and media figure, using structured data rather than comedic editing, and producing measurable coherence scores rather than audience laughter. **Practical application:**

## The Scoring Methodology Taxonomy (post-Batch 4)

Four named methodologies for scoring political profiles, to be applied based on subject characteristics:

1. **Standard whole-profile scoring.** Default. Score the subject on the 9 axes using their aggregate public record. Appropriate for subjects with stable trajectories and clearly identifiable current positions. Most profiles in the dataset.

2. **Primary-period scoping.** Score the subject on the period they are currently most well-known for, with divergent-content tracked in a caveat section. Appropriate for subjects whose recent trajectory is in-progress but has not yet displaced their primary identity. Canonical cases: Stewart (stable-across-25-years is the primary period), Peterson (2016–2020 pre-health-crisis is the primary period).

3. **Dual-profile methodology.** Score the subject as two (or more) distinct profiles covering substantively different frameworks, with a predictive-analysis section using one profile to evaluate actions from another period. Appropriate for subjects whose pre-political record is substantively different from their political record and whose blended score is artificially low. Canonical case: Trump (pre-2015 celebrity-businessman + 2016–2024 populist-nationalist + 2025/26 second-term predictive validation).

4. **Tenet-extraction.** Identify stable underlying tenets across the subject's public record, analyze apparent political drift as applications of those tenets to changing political contexts, separately track variant positions not generated by the tenets. Appropriate for subjects with significant pre-political records and ideologically-reversed-or-drifted trajectories where the drift is better explained by tenet-application than by whole-person movement. Canonical case: Musk (eight tenets, strongest predictive test via the pro-skilled-immigration position).

Future subjects should be reviewed against these four methodologies and scored with the one that best fits their structure.
